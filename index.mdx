Equinox is a native macOS app that lets you build **Dynamic Desktop** wallpapers — the same format Apple ships with macOS. Choose from three wallpaper types, drag in your images, and export a wallpaper file ready to use on any Mac running macOS Mojave or later.

## Three ways to make your wallpaper dynamic

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Solar" icon="sun" href="/wallpapers/solar">
    Images change based on the sun's position in the sky — perfect for landscape photos taken throughout the day.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Time" icon="clock" href="/wallpapers/time">
    Define a custom schedule and your wallpaper will cycle through images at the times you choose.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Appearance" icon="circle-half-stroke" href="/wallpapers/appearance">
    Two images — one for Light Mode, one for Dark Mode — that switch automatically with your system.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Get started

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download Equinox">
    Install Equinox from the [Mac App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/equinox-create-wallpaper/id1591510203) or download the latest release from [GitHub](https://github.com/rlxone/Equinox/releases).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Choose a wallpaper type">
    Open Equinox and select **Solar**, **Time**, or **Appearance** depending on how you want your wallpaper to change.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add your images">
    Drag and drop your photos into the gallery. PNG, JPEG, TIFF, and HEIC formats are all supported.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export and set">
    Click **Create** to export your wallpaper file, then right-click it and choose **Services → Set Desktop Picture** to apply it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Equinox requires macOS 10.14 Mojave or later. Dynamic Desktop features work best on macOS Catalina and newer.
</Note>
